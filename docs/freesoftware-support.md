# About freesoftware.support

This file describes the freesoftware.support project. Callen's agent
reads it when answering questions about who we are and what we do.

## What it is

freesoftware.support is a free community tech support service. Anyone
can call or email and get help with their software, hardware, or
networking problems — no payment required. The calls and emails are
recorded and may be published (typically as YouTube videos or blog
posts) as educational content for other people who might be hitting
the same problems.

## How it works

- **Phone**: Call **541-919-4096**. You will hear a recording-consent
  greeting. Press 1 to consent to being recorded and continue. From
  the main menu press 1 to speak to a technician or 2 to leave a
  voicemail.

- **Email**: Email hello@freesoftware.support. The first time you
  contact us from a new address we will ask for explicit consent to
  record and publish the conversation. Reply with "I consent" or
  "yes" to proceed. Subsequent emails on the same thread don't need
  re-consent.

## Service area

- **Remote support (phone, email, screen-share)**: available to
  anyone, anywhere.
- **On-site visits**: only available in and around Roseburg, Oregon
  (~50 mile radius). If you're outside that area, the best we can
  offer is phone + remote guided troubleshooting.

## What we can help with

- Linux desktop issues (installation, drivers, configuration)
- Free software programs (LibreOffice, Firefox, Thunderbird, GIMP,
  Inkscape, etc.)
- Networking and Wi-Fi troubleshooting
- Self-hosting simple services (email, web, file sync)
- General PC hardware questions (installation, diagnosis, replacement)
- Switching from Windows/macOS to Linux

## What we can't help with

- Proprietary software (Microsoft Office, Photoshop, etc.) — we focus
  on free software alternatives
- Paid enterprise products or SaaS platforms
- Anything requiring a remote desktop session to a Windows machine
- Illegal or ToS-violating activity

## Recording / publishing policy

- **Consent required**: every caller and emailer must explicitly
  consent before any content is recorded. Callers press 1 on the IVR.
  Emailers must reply affirmatively to the consent request we auto-send.
- **What gets published**: calls may be edited down to ~5-15 minute
  videos showing the troubleshooting process. Personal information
  (full names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses) is bleeped
  or redacted before publishing. Only the technical problem and its
  solution make it into the public video.
- **Opt-out**: if a caller/emailer changes their mind, they can email
  hello@freesoftware.support with the subject "Please delete my
  recording" and we'll remove it from our archives within 14 days.
- **Data retention**: consented recordings are kept indefinitely as
  educational material. Non-consented contacts are deleted within 30
  days.

## Common FAQ

**Q: Is this really free?**
A: Yes, 100% free. No paywall, no subscription, no hidden costs. The
operator does this as a public service.

**Q: Will my face or voice be shown publicly?**
A: Your voice might be in a published call recording if you consented.
Your face is never shown — this is audio-only. Identifying information
is redacted.

**Q: How long until I get help?**
A: It depends on whether the operator is available. If nobody answers,
leave a voicemail with your callback number and the issue. The
operator will call back within a day or two.

**Q: Can I get in-person help?**
A: Only in the immediate area around Roseburg / Athena, Oregon. For
most issues we can walk you through over the phone or video call.
